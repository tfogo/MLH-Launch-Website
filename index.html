About

Academia in Medicine

Academia relates to the community concerned with the pursuit of research, education, and scholarship. While the medical profession is founded on this philosophy, it can sometimes be difficult for trainees to access academic medicine, whether that be in clinical or laboratory research setting, in medical education, or even the more recently evolving fields of sports medicine or medical innovation and management.

The Conference

On Saturday 26th July, The Sir Alexander Fleming Building of Imperial College's South Kensington Campus will be hosting a one day national conference to encourage trainee involvement in medical academia. The morning session will consist of a series of expert-delivered talks on academia in clinical science, medical education and sport, with the afternoon assembly made up of seminars on the topics of:

  • Academic Career Paths in Medicine
  • Academic Job Applications
  • Statistics in Clinical Research
  • Postgraduate Qualifications in Medical Education
  • Information Technology in Medical Innovation

Speakers

Dr Tim Lancaster

Director of Clinical Studies • University of Oxford

Dr Tim Lancaster qualified in medicine at London University, after taking a degree in English. He trained in London and the USA. Since 1992, he has combined clinical practice in a city centre general practice with teaching and research. He currently is editor of the Cochrane Tobacco Addiction Review Group, and Director of Clinical Studies for the Oxford medical school.

Dr Fu Siong Ng

NIHR Clinical Lecturer in Cardiology • National Heart and Lung Institute

Dr Fu Siong Ng is an NIHR Clinical Lecturer in Cardiology at the National Heart and Lung Institute, and a Specialist Registrar in Cardiology at Imperial College Healthcare NHS Trust. He currently carries out research into arrhythmogenic mechanisms at Imperial College London, alongside clinical work in cardiac electrophysiology at Hammersmith Hospital and St. Mary's Hospital in London.
